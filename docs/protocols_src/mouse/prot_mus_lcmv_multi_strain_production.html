LCMV Production: Unified Multi-Strain Protocol (Armstrong, Clone 13, WE, Docile)

Abdullah Lab, IMMEI, University Hospital Bonn

Propagation on fibroblast cell lines and harvest of high-titer stocks.
Published

March 28, 2026

Protocol ID: MUS-LCMV-PROD-001
Version: v1.2
Author: Dillon Corvino

Purpose

This protocol describes a unified approach for in vitro propagation of lymphocytic choriomeningitis virus (LCMV) strains commonly used in mouse infection models:

  • LCMV Armstrong (ARM)
  • LCMV Clone 13 (Cl13)
  • LCMV WE
  • LCMV Docile (WE-derived persistent variant)

The procedure is based primarily on Welsh & Seedhom, Curr Protoc Microbiol 2008 (LCMV: Propagation, Quantitation, and Storage) and adapted using production conditions reported in high-impact immunology studies (e.g. Recher et al., Nat Med 2007; Bergthaler et al., PNAS 2010; Macal et al., Immunity 2018). The core infection and harvest workflow reflects the validated Cologne production protocol.

All strains are propagated on fibroblast-like cell lines (BHK-21 or L929) under BSL-2 conditions, with strain-specific preferences summarized below.

WarningLCMV biosafety and handling

LCMV is a zoonotic arenavirus capable of causing severe disease in humans, including meningitis and congenital infection. Work with live LCMV must:

  • Be conducted under the appropriate institutional biosafety level (typically BSL-2 with additional local conditions) and approved animal and biosafety protocols.
  • Use dedicated infectious CO2 incubators and clearly labelled containment for all cultures and waste.
  • Include suitable PPE (lab coat/gown, gloves, eye protection as appropriate) and measures to avoid sharps injuries, aerosol formation, and spills.
  • Exclude or take special precautions for pregnant or immunocompromised personnel according to institutional policies.

All waste (liquid and solid) must be inactivated according to local regulations (e.g. chemical disinfection and/or autoclaving) before disposal.

Warning

LCMV can cause meningitis and febrile illness in humans. Handle infectious material under BSL-2 conditions and according to your institutional biosafety guidelines.

Strains and Recommended Producer Cell Lines

LCMV can grow in many adherent cell lines, but fibroblast/epithelial lines give the best yields. BHK-21 cells are the standard for most strains; WE and Docile are also frequently grown on L929 cells.

Strain Phenotype in mice Parent strain Preferred producer cell line(s) Typical use
Armstrong Acute, self-limited — BHK-21 (also L929 possible) Acute infection model
Clone 13 Chronic systemic Armstrong BHK-21 (sometimes L929/BHK) Chronic infection, T cell exhaustion
WE Typically acute/visceral WE L929 or BHK-21 Acute / hemorrhagic-like models
Docile Chronic / persistent WE BHK-21 or L929 Chronic WE-derived infection

In practice:

  • Use BHK-21 as the default producer line for Armstrong and Clone 13.
  • Use L929 (or BHK-21) for WE.
  • For Docile, follow the same protocol as WE; if unsure, start with BHK-21 and check titers.

Overview of Workflow

  1. Detach, count, and seed producer cells into a T-75 at 5 x 10^6 cells in 15 mL.
  2. Allow 3–4 h for attachment.
  3. Remove 5 mL medium and infect at strain-specific MOI in remaining volume.
  4. Incubate 48 h.
  5. Harvest clarified supernatant carefully without disturbing the cell layer.
  6. Centrifuge, transfer to ice, aliquot at strain-specific volumes, and place directly at -80°C.
  7. Titrate by plaque assay (Vero cells) or focus-forming assay (MC57G or Vero).

Materials

Cell lines

BHK-21 (baby hamster kidney, Syrian hamster fibroblast-like)

  • Standard producer line for LCMV Armstrong, Clone 13, and Docile.
  • Maintenance medium: DMEM high glucose + 10% heat-inactivated FBS, +/- 1% Pen/Strep.
  • Fast-dividing; can undergo up to two divisions per day at 37°C, 5% CO2.
  • Use low-passage stocks; regularly test for mycoplasma.

L929 (mouse connective tissue fibroblast)

  • Preferred producer line for LCMV WE and Docile; also used for Armstrong and Clone 13.
  • Maintenance medium: DMEM + 5% FBS, +/- 1% Pen/Strep.
  • Grows more slowly than BHK-21; adjust seeding density and attachment time accordingly.
  • Use low-passage stocks; regularly test for mycoplasma.
Note

Omit Pen/Strep from the production medium during infection if desired, as antibiotics can mask low-level contamination. Ensure all reagents and technique are sterile before removing antibiotic cover.

Virus

  • Seed stocks of:
    • LCMV Armstrong
    • LCMV Clone 13
    • LCMV WE
    • LCMV Docile

Reagents and consumables

  • Phosphate-buffered saline (PBS), sterile
  • Trypsin-EDTA
  • Trypan blue (for cell counting)
  • 15 mL and 50 mL conical tubes
  • 10 mL stripettes
  • Cryovials for viral aliquots
  • Bleach (10% freshly prepared) and 70% ethanol for disinfection
  • Isopropanol freezing container (e.g. Mr. Frosty) — optional, see Freezing section

Equipment

  • Class II biological safety cabinet (laminar flow hood)
  • CO2 incubator (37°C, 5% CO2)
  • Tissue culture flasks (T-25, T-75, T-150, or T-175 depending on scale)
  • Tabletop centrifuge (capable of 1500 rpm / ~400 × g at 4°C)
  • Haemocytometer or automated cell counter
  • Inverted microscope
  • -80°C freezer

Producer Cell Preparation

All work in the biosafety cabinet.

  1. Detach producer cells (BHK-21 or L929) using trypsin-EDTA and collect into a conical tube.
  2. Centrifuge 5 min, 400 × g, 20°C.
  3. Aspirate supernatant. Resuspend in 5 mL medium and count: use 20 µL cells in 180 µL trypan blue (1:10 dilution).
  4. Seed cells in complete medium into the production flask — see scaling table below for cell numbers and volumes per flask size.
  5. Incubate at 37°C, 5% CO2 for 3–4 h to allow attachment.
Tip

At this seeding density, cells will be approximately 50% confluent after attachment — the optimal density for infection. Infecting fully confluent monolayers reduces viral yield.

Infection Conditions by Strain

Strain Producer line MOI (PFU/cell) Harvest time
Armstrong BHK-21 0.01 48 h
Clone 13 BHK-21 0.001 48 h
WE L929 (or BHK) 0.01 48 h
Docile BHK-21 / L929 0.01 48 h
Note

MOI reference: Values above reflect the validated Cologne production protocol for WE (MOI 0.01) and Clone 13 (MOI 0.001). Published literature and some institutional SOPs use higher MOIs for Clone 13 (0.05–0.1) and a broader range for other strains (0.01–0.1). Lower MOI with a fixed 48 h harvest limits DI particle accumulation and tends to give more consistent titers. If yields are low, increasing MOI to 0.01–0.1 is a reasonable first troubleshooting step.

Infection Procedure

All work in the biosafety cabinet.

  1. Thaw a virus aliquot rapidly at 37°C and place immediately on ice.
  2. Remove 5 mL of medium from the flask — do not disturb the cell layer.
  3. Dilute the appropriate volume of virus in 5 mL of medium to achieve the target MOI and add to the flask. Total volume is 15 mL. Gently tilt to distribute.
  4. Return flask to incubator. Incubate for 48 h at 37°C, 5% CO2 without further medium change.

Scaling to larger flasks

The Cologne protocol is defined for a T-75. Scale proportionally by surface area for larger flasks — cell number, seeding volume, and production volume all scale together. The 5 mL used to prepare the inoculum is always replaced with 5 mL fresh medium containing virus, so total volume is maintained throughout.

Flask Surface area Seeding cells Seeding volume Production volume Welsh & Seedhom reference volume
T-25 25 cm2 ~2 x 10^6 5 mL 5 mL ~10 mL
T-75 75 cm2 5 x 10^6 15 mL 15 mL 30–40 mL
T-150 150 cm2 ~10 x 10^6 30 mL 30 mL 60 mL
T-175 175 cm2 ~12 x 10^6 35 mL 35 mL ~70 mL
Note

Welsh & Seedhom use significantly larger production volumes (30–40 mL in a T-75), with a 1 h adsorption step followed by top-up to full volume. This gives more total PFU per run but a more dilute harvest. The Cologne volumes are lower and keep virion concentration in the harvest higher. For scale-up beyond T-175, topping up toward the Welsh & Seedhom volumes is reasonable to prevent nutrient limitation over 48 h.

Tip

Alternative adsorption approach (some protocols): Aspirate all medium, add inoculum in a minimal volume (3–5 mL for a T-75), incubate 1 h at 37°C rocking every 10–15 min, then top up to the full production volume. This maximises early virus-cell contact. The Cologne protocol avoids full aspiration — it removes only 5 mL and replaces with 5 mL containing virus, keeping cells submerged throughout.

Harvest

All work in the biosafety cabinet. Keep tubes on ice throughout.

  1. Using a 10 mL stripette, carefully remove all medium from the flask. Do not touch the cell layer.
  2. Transfer medium to a 15 or 50 mL conical tube.
  3. Centrifuge 5 min, 1500 rpm (~400 × g), 4°C to pellet cells and debris.
  4. Carefully transfer the clarified supernatant to a new Falcon tube and place on ice inside the hood.
Tip

Alternative clarification: Some protocols use 300–500 × g for 10 min at 4°C, followed by optional filtration through a 0.22 µm membrane. This gives cleaner supernatant but is not necessary for standard production runs. Avoid filtration if titer is borderline, as some infectivity may be lost.

Tip

Gently tapping the flask before harvest to dislodge loosely adherent/infected cells into the supernatant is an option used in some protocols; the Cologne approach avoids this to minimise cell debris in the harvest.

Aliquoting and Freezing

Aliquot volumes

Aliquot sizes are strain-specific, based on typical experimental dose requirements:

Strain Aliquot volume
WE 50 µL
Clone 13 200–300 µL
Armstrong 200–500 µL
Docile 200–300 µL
Tip

For high-titer stocks or when effectors are not limiting (e.g. cell-line-based production), larger aliquots (0.5–1 mL) may be practical. Adjust to match expected experimental doses and number of uses per vial.

Freezing

Snap-freeze aliquots in a dry ice / isopropanol bath, then transfer to -80°C for storage.

To prepare the bath:

  1. Add dry ice to a suitable container (e.g. a small polystyrene box or ice bucket — do not use glass or material that will crack at -78°C).
  2. Crush or break the dry ice into smaller pieces to maximise surface contact.
  3. Pour isopropanol (or 100% ethanol) over the dry ice until it covers the pieces. The bath will initially “boil” vigorously as CO2 sublimates — this is normal. Wait until bubbling slows before use (~1–2 min). The bath reaches approximately -78°C with isopropanol or ethanol; substituting acetone gives ~-86°C.
  4. Submerge cryovials in the bath. Small aliquots (50–500 µL) will be solid within ~30–60 seconds.
  5. Transfer frozen vials immediately to -80°C storage.
Warning

Use insulated gloves or forceps — the bath is -78°C. Work in a ventilated area or fume hood; isopropanol and ethanol vapour are flammable. Keep away from open flame.

Tip

Direct -80°C (Cologne approach): Placing aliquots directly into -80°C without a snap-freeze bath is simpler and used routinely in the Cologne protocol. Freezing rate at -80°C is slower (~-1°C to -5°C/min depending on vial placement and freezer load) but adequate for standard production runs where stocks contain 10% FBS as a cryoprotectant.

Tip

Controlled-rate alternative (Mr. Frosty): An isopropanol-based controlled-rate freezing container (e.g. Mr. Frosty) gives a defined -1°C/min rate — optimal for cells but unnecessarily slow for virus stocks and not recommended here.

Warning

Avoid repeated freeze–thaw cycles. Use multiple small aliquots and thaw each vial only once. Expect approximately 50% loss of infectivity per freeze–thaw even with FBS present.

Titration of Virus Stocks

  • Determine infectious titer using plaque assay on Vero cells or focus-forming assay on Vero or MC57G cells.
  • Record titers as PFU/mL (plaque assay) or FFU/mL (focus-forming assay).
  • Use the same assay type consistently across experiments.

Separate Quarto protocols exist for:

  • LCMV plaque assay on Vero cells (prot_mus_lcmv_focus_forming_assay.qmd — adapt as needed).
  • LCMV focus-forming assay (immunofocus) on MC57G or Vero cells.

Storage of LCMV Stocks

  • Store viral stocks at -70 to -80°C.
  • Include a protein stabilizer (e.g. 10% FBS in the viral supernatant) to improve freeze–thaw stability.
  • Highly purified, protein-poor preparations are extremely unstable on freeze–thaw.
  • When thawing: thaw rapidly at 37°C, then immediately transfer to ice for all downstream handling.

Troubleshooting

Problem Possible cause Suggested solution
Very low titer Cells too confluent or unhealthy; MOI too low Confirm ~50% confluency at infection; try MOI 0.05–0.1; check mycoplasma
Massive cell detachment Infection too long or MOI too high Shorten to 48 h; reduce MOI
Inconsistent titers Variable cell density or incubation time Standardise seeding density (5 x 10^6 / T-75) and harvest time
Suspected contamination Poor aseptic technique Review BSL-2 practices; discard contaminated stock and repeat

Strain-Specific Notes

Armstrong

  • Classical acute strain.
  • Propagated on BHK-21 cells at MOI 0.01 for 48 h.
  • Frequently used to generate high-titer stocks for i.p. or i.v. infections in the 2 x 10^5–2 x 10^6 PFU range.

Clone 13

  • Armstrong-derived chronic strain with enhanced visceral tropism.
  • Cologne MOI: 0.001 — lower than many published protocols (0.05–0.1) to reduce DI particle burden and improve stock quality.
  • For chronic infection models, typical i.v. doses are 2 x 10^6 to 2 x 10^7 PFU depending on experimental design.

WE

  • Viscerotropic; commonly used as an acute systemic infection model.
  • Propagated on L929 cells, though BHK-21 also supports growth.
  • For hemorrhagic-like disease models, stock quality and titer consistency are critical.

Docile

  • WE-derived persistent variant.
  • Grows on BHK-21 and L929 cells; replication in vitro may be slightly slower than WE in some systems.
  • Used for chronic infection models as an alternative to Clone 13.

Safety Considerations

  • Confirm that all work with LCMV and infected animals/samples is covered by approved biosafety and animal use protocols.
  • Perform all live-virus manipulations in a certified Class II biosafety cabinet.
  • Use dedicated infectious incubators and clearly label flasks and waste containers with strain and date.
  • Wear appropriate PPE at all times (lab coat/gown, gloves, eye/face protection as required).
  • Decontaminate all liquid waste (e.g. freshly prepared 10% bleach) before disposal. Solid waste must be autoclaved or inactivated per institutional guidelines.
  • Pregnant or immunocompromised personnel should not work with LCMV unless explicitly allowed under institutional policy with additional safeguards.
