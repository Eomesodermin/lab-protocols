Mouse Spleen Dissociation to Single-Cell Suspension

Abdullah Lab, IMMEI, University Hospital Bonn

Preparation of mouse spleen single-cell suspensions for flow cytometry, sorting, and downstream immunological assays.
Published

November 26, 2025

Protocol ID: MUS-SPL-DIS-001
Version: v1.1
Author: Dillon Corvino

Purpose

This protocol describes the rapid isolation of leukocytes from mouse spleen using mechanical dissociation, red blood cell lysis, and gentle washing steps. The resulting suspension is suitable for flow cytometry, sorting, and standard immunological assays.

Critical notes (read before starting)

  • Work on ice or at 4 °C after dissociation to preserve viability.
  • Do not reuse strainers between spleens.
  • RBC lysis should be time-limited (5 minutes) to prevent leukocyte damage.

Approximate timing

  • Dissection and spleen removal: 2–3 min
  • Mechanical dissociation: 2–3 min
  • RBC lysis: 5 min
  • Final wash and resuspension: 5 min

Total hands-on time per spleen: 15–20 minutes.

Procedure

STEP 1 – Collection and preparation

  1. Euthanise the mouse by an approved method.
  2. Expose the spleen by opening the abdominal cavity.
  3. Excise the spleen and remove connective tissue & adipose tissue
  4. Place it into a petri dish or 15 mL Falcon tube containing cold PBS or FACS Buffer.

STEP 2 – Mechanical dissociation

  1. Place a 70 µm cell strainer over a 50 mL Falcon tube.
  2. Transfer the spleen onto the strainer.
  3. Using the back of a 5 mL syringe plunger, gently mash the spleen through the mesh until a homogenous suspension is obtained.
  4. Rinse the strainer with FACS Buffer and top the tube up to 50 mL.
Note

Max of 2 spleens per tube/strainer

STEP 3 – Wash and centrifugation

  1. Centrifuge the 50 mL tube at 400 g for 5 minutes at 4 °C.
  2. Carefully pour off the supernatant.

STEP 4 – RBC lysis

  1. Add 1 mL HybriMax RBC Lysis Buffer (or equivalent) per spleen to the pellet.
  2. Gently resuspend the pellet by pipetting or flicking.
  3. Incubate for 5 minutes at room temperature.
  4. Top up to 50 mL with FACS Buffer.
Warning

Avoid over-lysis (>5 minutes) as this can reduce leukocyte viability.

STEP 5 – Filtration and final wash

  1. Filter the suspension through a fresh 70 µm strainer into a clean tube.
  2. Centrifuge at 400 g for 5 minutes at 4 °C.
  3. Carefully discard supernatant.
  4. Resuspend the pellet in 5 mL FACS Buffer (or a volume appropriate for your downstream assay).

STEP 6 – Downstream handling

Proceed immediately to:

  • Flow cytometry staining
  • FACS sorting
  • Cryopreservation
  • Functional assays

Keep cells on ice until use.

Buffers used

  • FACS Buffer – BUF-FACS-v1.0
  • RBC Lysis Buffer (HybriMax) – BUF-RBC-HYB-v1.0

Troubleshooting

Issue Possible cause Suggested solution
Low cell yield Incomplete mechanical dissociation Spend additional time mashing through mesh
Excess debris Over-aggressive mashing Apply gentler pressure
Poor viability Prolonged room-temperature handling Keep samples cold; minimise processing time
Pellets lost Pouring too forcefully Use slow, controlled decanting

Safety (brief)

  • Process mouse tissues according to S1/S2 guidelines and ethics approval.
  • RBC lysis buffer contains toxic reagents — wear PPE and avoid inhalation or skin contact.
  • Dispose of biological material and sharps appropriately.
